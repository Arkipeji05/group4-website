Philippines Pandemic Of Poverty

MANILA - This paper's editorial (“Poor yet to feel fruits of GDP”) last Monday gave us a clear picture of something wrong that's happening in our country's economic trajectory. When viewed from the top, our economy is on a “high growth path.” But when assessed on the ground, the “high growth” describes instead our very alarming level of poverty. The National Economic and Development Authority trumpeted that our economy grew by 6.4 percent in the first three months of this year, beating many countries in the Asian region. This reportedly extends the uninterrupted period of growth of our economy that began in the second quarter of 2021.
